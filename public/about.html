About Me

I’m an engineering student who enjoys building systems where multiple components have to work together cleanly for anything meaningful to happen. Most of my work involves connecting backend services, working with data and models, and thinking carefully about how software behaves once it’s running — how it handles state, responds to change, and performs under real constraints.

I’m particularly interested in how technology behaves outside ideal environments: systems interacting with hardware, models operating with limited resources, and software that needs to stay reliable without constant external support. I like exploring trade-offs, iterating on implementations, and refining ideas until they work in practice — not just in theory.
